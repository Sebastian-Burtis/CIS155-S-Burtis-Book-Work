In Part 5 of JoJo's Bizarre Adventure, titled "Golden Wind," Diavolo and Doppio serve as the primary antagonists. They are actually two personalities inhabiting the same body. Diavolo is the secretive and enigmatic boss of the powerful Italian mafia organization Passione. He operates under the alias "The Boss" and is feared and respected by all who serve him. Diavolo is obsessed with maintaining his anonymity and will go to extreme lengths to eliminate anyone who threatens to expose his true identity. Doppio, on the other hand, is Diavolo's alternate personality. Doppio is portrayed as innocent, childlike, and easily manipulated. He serves as Diavolo's loyal subordinate and unwittingly carries out his orders while remaining unaware of Diavolo's existence within him. The conflict in Part 5 revolves around Giorno Giovanna, a young man with a connection to the Joestar family, who joins Passione with the goal of overthrowing Diavolo and reforming the mafia from within. As Giorno rises through the ranks of Passione, he encounters both Doppio and Diavolo, ultimately uncovering their true nature and their connection to each other. Throughout the story, Diavolo and Doppio engage in a complex game of cat and mouse with Giorno and his allies, leading to numerous intense battles and confrontations. Diavolo's Stand, "King Crimson," grants him the ability to manipulate time, allowing him to erase moments from reality and predict his opponents' actions. The conflict between Diavolo, Doppio, and Giorno culminates in a final showdown, where Giorno and his allies must overcome Diavolo's formidable powers and put an end to his reign of terror over Passione. Diavolo and Doppio are remembered as two of the most compelling and mysterious characters in JoJo's Bizarre Adventure, known for their complex personalities, unique abilities, and their role in the intricate plot of Part 5.
